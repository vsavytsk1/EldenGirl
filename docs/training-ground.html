The one trick
this refuses

design note

Casino games, gacha games and infinite feeds run on variable-ratio reinforcement: a reward that arrives on an unpredictable schedule. Skinner showed it produces the most persistent behaviour there is, and the hardest to extinguish.

That is the exact mechanism this game is teaching you to recognise. Cruelty, then tenderness, never on a schedule you can predict — that is what makes leaving feel like dying. It is the engine of trauma bonding.

So it would be obscene to build the lesson on the weapon.

Everything here is earned and predictable. The multiplier is shown before you answer. Streak thresholds are fixed and visible. There are no random rewards, no loot boxes, no manufactured scarcity, no near-miss theatre, no timer pressuring you, and nothing that punishes you for closing the tab.

Juice without the hook. You should always be able to say exactly why you got what you got — which is, not by accident, the same standard the guidebook asks you to hold a person to.

And Mochi? A reacting creature borrows the caregiving instinct, which is real and strong and cheap. It is a nudge, it is disclosed, and it is pointed at something that is actually good for you. A technique is not automatically a manipulation — what makes it one is hiding it. So it is not hidden.
