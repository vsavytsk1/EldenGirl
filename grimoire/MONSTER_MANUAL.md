## RULE 0 · WHY THIS SCROLL EXISTS

The whole point is to pass the moveset to the next girl before it is used on her. A
tactic hoarded rots; a tactic named loses its power. These behaviours work because
they are **unnamed** — formless wrongness routes back to *"something is wrong with
me."* Name it and it stops being weather and becomes a **moveset** you can read.

This is the **core content doc** for the whole project. It is where every concept
starts: we distil a monster here, test it as **text and clicks** in the concept lab
(`docs/`), and only then does it earn the UI layers and, later, the secure native
app. **The manual is the source; the builds are its renders.**

> This is a bestiary, not a diagnosis. A manual cannot assess your relationship or
> your danger. It gives you the **patterns** and lets you do the judging — that is the
> whole argument. If you are in danger now, a person is faster than any app: advocates
> do safety planning free. **US 1-800-799-7233 · UK 0808 2000 247 · ES 016 · AR 144 ·
> GR 15900 · EU 116 006.**

## WHY TWELVE

There are **12 monsters** because the soul that holds fewer falls apart and the one
that holds more cannot see them all at once. Twelve is the working set: the training
ground already carries exactly these twelve, and the count is not decoration — it is
the structure of a moveset a frightened mind can actually learn under stress.

**χ = 2** is the second law of this cave, and it is the *counter-curse itself*: every
loop must close. A tactic works by leaving a loop open in you — an unanswered *"was
that my fault?"* that runs as a background process forever. Naming the monster
**closes the loop**. That is the whole mechanic, in the training ground and in life.

## HOW TO READ A MONSTER

Every entry has the same six fields. This structure is the schema the concept-lab
builds read from — keep it stable.

| field | what it is |
|---|---|
| **THE MONSTER** | the tactic's name — the word that ends its power |
| **THE MOVE** | what it does to you, in one plain line |
| **THE TELL** | how to recognise it from the inside, where it is hardest to see |
| **THE LINES** | real phrases it speaks (drawn from the training ground data) |
| **THE COUNTER** | the named recognition that closes the loop — *not* confrontation |
| **THE ROOT** | the grounded source: whose research names this, so it is not folklore |

> **THE CLEAN LINE.** Not everything that stings is a monster. A mind that sees
> manipulation everywhere has swapped one distortion for another. The tell of a *clean*
> line is always the same three things: **specific**, **verifiable**, and **it owns
> itself** (the speaker's feeling is theirs to manage, not your fault). Round 2 of the
> training ground teaches this on purpose, because false positives are their own harm.

# PART I · THE TWELVE MONSTERS

## 1 · GASLIGHTING
- **THE MOVE:** Denying your perception until you stop trusting it.
- **THE TELL:** The target is your *instruments*. With no instruments, no measurement
  is possible — you stop being able to say what happened, so you stop objecting.
- **THE LINES:** *"That never happened, you're making it up." · "You're remembering it
  wrong. Again." · "You're being crazy — listen to yourself." · "Are you writing this
  down? That's insane."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name it silently: *this is gaslighting.* Keep your own record
  (offline, private — this is what the app's record tier is for). The written note is
  the instrument he is trying to break.
- **THE ROOT:** Bancroft; the nocebo/perception literature. The term is from the 1938
  play *Gas Light*.

## 2 · DARVO
- **THE MOVE:** Deny, Attack, Reverse Victim and Offender. You raise a harm; he denies
  it, attacks you for raising it, and becomes the wronged party. You end up apologising.
- **THE TELL:** Within one exchange, the roles invert. You came in hurt and leave
  guilty, with no idea how the handover happened.
- **THE LINES:** *"I can't believe you'd accuse me, after everything I do for you." ·
  "So now I'm the villain? Wow." · "You're the one hurting me by bringing this up." ·
  "I'm the one who should be upset here."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name the three beats as they land — *deny, attack, reverse.* Seeing
  the sequence stops the handover of guilt.
- **THE ROOT:** Jennifer Freyd, who named DARVO (betrayal-trauma research).

## 3 · MOVING THE GOALPOSTS
- **THE MOVE:** The standard changes the moment it is met.
- **THE TELL:** The impossibility **is** the function. It is not that you keep failing;
  it is that success is defined to be unreachable, to keep you working.
- **THE LINES:** *"I said clean, not just tidy." · "Sure you did it, but not right." ·
  "It doesn't count if I had to ask." · "I shouldn't have to tell you what I need."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name that the target moved. The work was done; the goal was
  retracted. That is the monster, not your inadequacy.
- **THE ROOT:** Stark (coercive control as a pattern of impossible, shifting demands).

## 4 · FUTURE FAKING
- **THE MOVE:** The change that is always arriving and never arrives.
- **THE TELL:** It costs nothing to promise and buys real time. The promise is always
  pinned to a condition that never resolves.
- **THE LINES:** *"I'll go to therapy, I promise — just give me time." · "Once things
  calm down at work." · "When we move, everything gets better." · "One more chance, the
  last one, I swear."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name it, and watch **behaviour, not words.** A clean promise is
  specific and verifiable (a booked appointment with a name); a fake one is a horizon.
- **THE ROOT:** Cialdini (commitment/consistency, weaponised); Bancroft.

## 5 · ISOLATION
- **THE MOVE:** Removing every outside reference point — friends, family, money, work.
- **THE TELL:** The **load-bearing tactic**: every other monster depends on it. Cut off
  from other instruments, you have only his readings to go by.
- **THE LINES:** *"Your friend doesn't respect me — I don't want you seeing her." ·
  "Your family is toxic, they're turning you against me." · "Why do you need anyone
  else? You've got me." · "We don't need your money — quit the job."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name it, and **keep one thread.** One friend, one account, one door.
  Isolation is the tactic advocates ask about first, because it is the one that traps.
- **THE ROOT:** Stark (isolation as the core of the "condition of unfreedom").

## 6 · LOVE BOMBING
- **THE MOVE:** Overwhelming early intensity that creates a debt.
- **THE TELL:** Cialdini's reciprocity, weaponised. The gift is enormous, early, and
  **called in later** — the intensity is the down-payment on control.
- **THE LINES:** *"You're the only one who's ever really understood me." · "I've never
  felt like this about anyone, ever." · "I knew from day one — we're meant to be." ·
  "Move in with me now. Why wait?"*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name the speed. Real intimacy is slow and does not invoice you.
  Intensity is not depth.
- **THE ROOT:** Cialdini (reciprocity); the cult "love-bomb" literature.

## 7 · HOOVERING
- **THE MOVE:** The pull-back after you leave.
- **THE TELL:** Reliably the **warmest he has ever been** — arriving at exactly the
  moment of maximum leverage, when you are almost out.
- **THE LINES:** *"I've changed, I've been in therapy, I'm not that person." · "I can't
  live without you — I mean it this time." · "Just one coffee. That's all." · "I'm in
  hospital, I didn't know who else to call."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name the timing. The warmth is a function of your distance, not his
  change. This is the moment to talk to an advocate, not to him.
- **THE ROOT:** The variable-ratio reinforcement schedule (Skinner) — see Part II.

## 8 · MANUFACTURED WORTHLESSNESS
- **THE MOVE:** Making the exit look like a cliff.
- **THE TELL:** Cialdini's scarcity, pointed at **your self-image**. The scarce resource
  is made to be *you* — no one else would want you, so the door is a drop.
- **THE LINES:** *"Nobody else would put up with you." · "You'd be alone — is that what
  you want?" · "Who else wants you, at your age?" · "Good luck out there with two kids."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name it as a **sales tactic**, not a truth. Manufactured scarcity is
  a technique; your worth is not his to price.
- **THE ROOT:** Cialdini (scarcity); Bancroft.

## 9 · SUNK COST
- **THE MOVE:** The years already spent, used as a lock.
- **THE TELL:** The past is made to buy the future. The more you have endured, the more
  endurance is demanded, as if leaving would "waste" the suffering.
- **THE LINES:** *"After everything we've been through, you'd throw it away?" · "Five
  years — you'd waste five years?" · "We've come too far to quit now." · "You'd break up
  the family over one argument?"*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name the fallacy: spent time is **already gone** and cannot be
  recovered by spending more. The only question is the next day, not the past ones.
- **THE ROOT:** Cialdini (commitment/consistency); the sunk-cost fallacy.

## 10 · TRIANGULATION
- **THE MOVE:** A third party installed as competitor, judge, or audience.
- **THE TELL:** Removes you as the only reference and installs a rival standard — an
  ex, a mother, "everyone at work" — always agreeing with him.
- **THE LINES:** *"My ex never had a problem with this." · "Even your own mother agrees
  with me." · "Everyone at work thinks you're unreasonable." · "Funny, nobody else
  finds me difficult."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name the third chair. The audience is usually invented or coached;
  the comparison exists to move the goalposts (see #3) with a borrowed voice.
- **THE ROOT:** Cialdini (social proof, weaponised); family-systems literature.

## 11 · MINIMISING
- **THE MOVE:** Shrinking the harm until raising it looks absurd.
- **THE TELL:** It teaches you to **pre-shrink your own experience.** That is where
  self-gaslighting starts — you apologise for mentioning it before he has to.
- **THE LINES:** *"It wasn't that bad, you're being dramatic." · "It was a joke — can't
  you take a joke?" · "Other people have it so much worse." · "I barely touched you."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name it, and **do not shrink the entry.** Write it at full size. The
  minimising is the monster; your measure was correct.
- **THE ROOT:** Herman (trauma and the silencing of the account); Bancroft.

## 12 · MANUFACTURED CHAOS
- **THE MOVE:** Instability so constant there is no bandwidth left to plan.
- **THE TELL:** A mind in permanent triage **cannot plan an exit** — that is the point.
  The rules change overnight; you spend all your energy managing his moods.
- **THE LINES:** *"Everything's a crisis and it's always your fault somehow." · "The
  rules changed again and nobody told you." · "You never know which version is coming
  through the door." · "The silence goes on for days and nobody tells you why."*
- **THE COUNTER:** Name the chaos as **engineered**, not accidental. Protect a small,
  quiet slice of bandwidth — that slice is where a safety plan gets made.
- **THE ROOT:** Stark (coercive control creates a "condition of unfreedom"); Herman.

# PART II · THE ONE TRICK THIS PROJECT REFUSES

Casinos, gacha games and infinite feeds run on **variable-ratio reinforcement**: a
reward on an *unpredictable* schedule. Skinner showed it produces the most persistent,
hardest-to-extinguish behaviour there is.

**That is the exact mechanism the abuse teaches by.** Cruelty, then tenderness, never
on a schedule you can predict — that is what makes leaving feel like dying. It is the
engine of trauma bonding, and it is why Hoovering (#7) lands.

> So it would be obscene to build the lesson on the weapon.

Every reward in every EldenGirl build is **earned and predictable**: the multiplier is
shown *before* you answer, thresholds are fixed and visible, there are no random
payouts, no loot boxes, no timers, no near-miss theatre. **Juice without the hook.**
You should always be able to say exactly why you got what you got — which is, not by
accident, the same standard this manual asks you to hold a person to. (This is a hard
gate, not a preference. See `docs/THE_APP.md` Part IV: *deterministic rewards only.*)

# PART III · THE COUNTER-CURSE IS RECOGNITION, NOT CONFRONTATION

The science is grounded and it is modest. **Inoculation** ("prebunking") — pre-exposing
someone to a weakened form of a tactic plus its refutation — is the best-validated
defence (McGuire; Banas & Rains 2010, mean d ≈ 0.43; the Cambridge prebunking work).
Two ingredients make it work: **naming the manipulative intent**, and **dispelling the
illusion of invulnerability** (Sagarin et al. 2002 — resistance only generalised once
people were shown they *personally* had just been fooled).

What this means for every build:

1. **Teach the tactic, not the verdict.** Technique-recognition generalises; case-by-case
   "is he abusive?" scoring does not, and a false negative is dangerous (banned — see
   `docs/THE_APP.md` Part V).
2. **Recognition is silent and internal.** The goal is to name the move *in your own
   head.* **Do not use this to win arguments with someone dangerous** — confronting an
   abuser with their own moveset can escalate risk. This warning ships on every screen.
3. **Resistance decays; dose it.** Inoculation fades in weeks without boosters. So the
   training ground is meant to be replayed — repetition is the mechanism, not a
   consolation prize.
4. **Point outward.** Every screen ends at a person: an advocate, a friend, a line.
   The manual makes patterns nameable and a number reachable. Leaving safely takes
   people.

# PART IV · FROM MONSTER TO BUILD — THE CONCEPT-LAB PIPELINE

The manual is the source of truth. Concepts flow from here to a shippable render on a
fast cycle:

```
1  MONSTER_MANUAL.md   the monster is written here first — moveset, tell, counter, root
2  docs/*.html         built as pure text + clicks: one file, zero deps, ZERO bytes written
3  the community        opens the URL, reads every line, judges the concept and the path
4  the UI layers        only a concept that holds earns the juice / the creature / the game
5  the secure app       and only then is it wrapped in the disguised, encrypted native tier
```

**Current renders**
- `docs/index.html` — the concept lab (the front door).
- `docs/training-ground.html` — all 12 monsters as the recognition drill (rounds 1–3,
  the clean line, the chain, the tell). Mochi comes along.

**Candidate renders (next in the lab)**
- **The Codex** — this manual's Part I as a plain, scrollable reference page.
- **The Escape Hatch** — the device-safety primer + offline helpline directory
  (region from locale only, never location — `docs/THE_APP.md` S9).
- **The Chain** — a build for the *sequences* (e.g. Isolation → Manufactured
  Worthlessness → Sunk Cost), because monsters hunt in packs.

**The invariants every render inherits** (the gates enforce these — `docs/THE_GATES.md`):
- writes **0 bytes** — no storage, no cookies, no service worker (G4)
- **no network** — works fully offline, nothing phones home (S8)
- **no install trace** — no manifest, handed over as a URL
- **zero dependencies** — self-contained HTML + inline JS
- deterministic rewards only — never the variable-ratio hook (Part II)
